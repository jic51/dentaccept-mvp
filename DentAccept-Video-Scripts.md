## VIDEO 9: DENTAL VENEER

**Medically verified sources:**
- ADA cosmetic dentistry patient guide
- American Academy of Cosmetic Dentistry (AACD) veneer standards

**Key procedure facts:**
- Veneers are ultra-thin shells (0.5–1mm) of porcelain or composite bonded to tooth front
- Types: porcelain (lasts 15–20 years), composite resin (lasts 5–7 years), no-prep (minimal or no enamel removal)
- Duration: 2 visits over 1–2 weeks (porcelain) or 1 visit (composite)
- Step 1: Consultation + shade selection
- Step 2: Minimal enamel removal (0.5mm) for traditional porcelain
- Step 3: Digital impressions sent to lab
- Step 4: Temporary veneers placed
- Step 5: Permanent veneers bonded with resin cement
- Step 6: UV curing + polishing
- Not suitable for: severe misalignment, teeth grinding (bruxism), thin enamel
- Cost: $1,000–$2,500 per tooth (porcelain); $250–$1,500 (composite)
