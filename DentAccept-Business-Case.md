## 1. THE PROBLEM: $200,000-$300,000 Lost Per Practice, Per Year

### The Data

The average general dentist in the U.S. generates **$942,290 in annual gross billings** (ADA Health Policy Institute, Survey of Dental Practice 2024). But a staggering portion of that potential revenue never materializes because patients don't accept recommended treatments.

**Case acceptance rates are declining:**

- In 2025, only **46% of dentists** reported that the majority of their complex treatment plans are accepted — down from 54% in 2024 and 57% in 2023. This is the lowest rate recorded since Inside Dentistry began tracking in 2019.
- Two-thirds of U.S. dental practices fall between **20% and 50% case acceptance**, well below the industry goal of 90%.
- The average practice closes only about **34 cents of every dollar** diagnosed. For every $100,000 in diagnosed treatment, roughly $66,000 never gets scheduled.

**What this means in real dollars:**

A practice billing $942,290/year with a 40% acceptance rate is collecting ~$377,000 from treatments patients accept. The remaining ~$565,000 in diagnosed work is declined or delayed. Even capturing just 30-40% of that gap — through better communication, not more marketing — represents **$170,000-$225,000 in recoverable revenue per year**.

### Why Patients Say No

Patients don't reject treatment because they don't need it. They reject it because of three psychological barriers:

**1. "I don't understand what you're telling me."**
Research shows patients only remember **20% of what doctors explain verbally**. The human brain processes visual information 60,000 times faster than text. When a dentist says "you need a root canal because the bacteria have reached the pulp chamber," the patient hears noise. They can't picture what's inside their own mouth.

**2. "It costs too much."**
Without understanding the consequences of waiting, patients compare the cost of treatment to zero — not to the cost of the problem getting worse. A $1,200 crown today sounds expensive until you see that waiting turns it into a $3,500 root canal + crown + bone graft.

**3. "Let me think about it."**
This is the most dangerous response. **Up to 60% of new patients never return after their first visit.** Among patients who leave without scheduling, approximately **40% never come back** for the recommended treatment. The decision window closes the moment they walk out the door.

## 2. THE SCIENCE: Why Visual Education Changes Everything

### Behavioral Economics — Loss Aversion

The core psychological principle behind DentAccept is **loss aversion**, one of the most well-documented findings in behavioral economics. Originally described by Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky, loss aversion demonstrates that **the psychological pain of losing something is approximately twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining something equivalent**.

Applied to dental treatment decisions:

- Telling a patient "this crown will protect your tooth" (framing as a gain) is far less motivating than showing them "here is what happens to your tooth over the next 12 months if you don't get the crown" (framing as avoiding a loss).
- Research in healthcare behavioral economics confirms that framing treatment compliance in terms of **what patients could lose by NOT acting** is significantly more effective than focusing only on benefits.

DentAccept's "What Happens If You Wait" module is built directly on this science. It doesn't just show the procedure — it shows the **consequences of inaction** with a visual timeline and escalating costs. This converts abstract fear into concrete understanding.

### The Evidence for Visual Patient Education

Multiple peer-reviewed studies demonstrate that visual tools dramatically improve both understanding and treatment acceptance:

- **97.6% of dental patients** prefer video-based education over brochures or posters, and **95.1%** agreed that videos improved their understanding (Westmead Centre for Oral Health study).
- Patients exposed to **3D animations** had significantly higher recall and were **3.91 times more likely** to answer questions about their treatment correctly compared to 2D presentations (Sibar Institute, South India).
- Recall improves from **20% (verbal only) to 50% (with visual aids)** — a 2.5x improvement in patient comprehension.
- Research from the **University of Pennsylvania** dental school shows patients presented with AR treatment simulations exhibit **34% higher retention** of treatment plan details compared to traditional verbal explanations.

### Case Acceptance Improvement

When practices implement visual chairside education tools:

- AR and visual patient education systems have been shown to boost case acceptance to **78%**, compared to the industry average of 46%.
- A VideaHealth study with DSO Lumio Dental documented **20%+ increase** in restorative treatment acceptance when AI-assisted visual presentations were used.
- Overjet reports that practices using AI-powered visual explanations see measurable increases in case acceptance because patients can **see and understand** what they previously couldn't.

## 3. THE SOLUTION: How DentAccept Works

DentAccept is a web-based visual presentation tool designed for chairside use on any tablet. Here's how it solves each of the three patient objections:

### Problem: "I don't understand" --> Solution: 20-Second Visual Animations

Instead of explaining a crown procedure verbally, the dental assistant opens DentAccept on an iPad, selects "Crown," and hands the tablet to the patient. They watch a clear, step-by-step animation showing:

1. What the problem looks like (decay, crack, infection)
2. What happens during the procedure (numbing, preparation, placement)
3. What the restored tooth looks like

**Why this works:** The brain processes images 60,000x faster than words. Seeing the procedure makes it real in a way that verbal explanations cannot achieve.

### Problem: "It's too expensive" --> Solution: The "What If You Wait" Module

After the procedure animation, DentAccept shows patients a **consequences timeline**:

- **Today:** Simple crown ($1,200)
- **6 months:** Decay spreads, may need root canal ($2,400)
- **1 year:** Tooth fracture, extraction + implant ($4,500)
- **2 years:** Bone loss, complex surgery ($8,000+)

This is loss aversion in action. The patient doesn't just see the cost of the treatment — they see the **escalating cost of doing nothing**. The $1,200 crown stops looking expensive and starts looking like a bargain.

### Problem: "Let me think about it" --> Solution: Decision at Chairside

By presenting the visual information right there in the operatory, while the patient is in the chair, DentAccept collapses the decision-making timeline. The patient:

1. **Sees** what's wrong (animation)
2. **Understands** the consequences (timeline)
3. **Decides** before leaving (scheduling prompt)

No brochure to take home and forget. No verbal explanation to misremember. The entire presentation takes under 2 minutes.

## 2. LA CIENCIA: Por Que La Educacion Visual Lo Cambia Todo

### Economia del Comportamiento — Aversion a la Perdida

El principio psicologico central detras de DentAccept es la **aversion a la perdida**, uno de los hallazgos mas documentados en economia del comportamiento. Descrito originalmente por Daniel Kahneman y Amos Tversky, la aversion a la perdida demuestra que **el dolor psicologico de perder algo es aproximadamente el doble de poderoso que el placer de ganar algo equivalente**.

Aplicado a decisiones de tratamiento dental:

- Decirle a un paciente "esta corona protegera su diente" (enmarcando como ganancia) es mucho menos motivador que mostrarle "esto es lo que le pasa a su diente en los proximos 12 meses si no se pone la corona" (enmarcando como evitar una perdida).
- La investigacion en economia del comportamiento en salud confirma que enmarcar el cumplimiento del tratamiento en terminos de **lo que los pacientes podrian perder al NO actuar** es significativamente mas efectivo que enfocarse solo en beneficios.

El modulo "Que Pasa Si Esperas" de DentAccept esta construido directamente sobre esta ciencia. No solo muestra el procedimiento — muestra las **consecuencias de la inaccion** con una linea de tiempo visual y costos escalantes. Esto convierte el miedo abstracto en comprension concreta.

### La Evidencia de la Educacion Visual del Paciente

Multiples estudios revisados por pares demuestran que las herramientas visuales mejoran dramaticamente tanto la comprension como la aceptacion del tratamiento:

- El **97.6% de los pacientes dentales** prefieren educacion basada en video sobre folletos o posters, y el **95.1%** estuvo de acuerdo en que los videos mejoraron su comprension (estudio del Westmead Centre for Oral Health).
- Pacientes expuestos a **animaciones 3D** tuvieron un recuerdo significativamente mayor y fueron **3.91 veces mas propensos** a responder preguntas sobre su tratamiento correctamente comparado con presentaciones 2D (Instituto Sibar, India).
- El recuerdo mejora del **20% (solo verbal) al 50% (con ayudas visuales)** — una mejora de 2.5 veces en la comprension del paciente.
- Investigacion de la **Universidad de Pennsylvania** muestra que pacientes presentados con simulaciones visuales de tratamiento exhiben un **34% mayor retencion** de los detalles del plan de tratamiento comparado con explicaciones verbales tradicionales.

### Mejora en Aceptacion de Casos

Cuando los consultorios implementan herramientas de educacion visual chairside:

- Los sistemas de educacion visual del paciente han demostrado impulsar la aceptacion de casos al **78%**, comparado con el promedio de la industria del 46%.
- Un estudio de VideaHealth con la cadena Lumio Dental documento un **aumento del 20%+** en aceptacion de tratamiento restaurativo cuando se usaron presentaciones visuales asistidas por IA.
